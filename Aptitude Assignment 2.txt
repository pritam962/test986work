1)Initial milk-water ratio: 2:3
Final milk-water ratio: 4:11
Quantity of milk in the mixture: 40 liters
Based on the initial milk-water ratio of 2:3, we can calculate the initial quantity of water in the mixture:

Initial quantity of water = (3/2) * Quantity of milk
= (3/2) * 40 liters
= 60 liters

Similarly, based on the final milk-water ratio of 4:11, we can calculate the final quantity of water in the mixture:

Final quantity of water = (11/4) * Quantity of milk
= (11/4) * 40 liters
= 110 liters

To find the quantity of water to be added, we subtract the initial quantity of water from the final quantity of water:

Quantity of water to be added = Final quantity of water - Initial quantity of water
= 110 liters - 60 liters
= 50 liters
